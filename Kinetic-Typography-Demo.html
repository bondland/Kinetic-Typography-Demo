Not for the wow factor

When text responds to scroll, visitors tend to stay longer. Not because of flashy effects, but because the experience feels more engaging and respectful of their time.

Motion with meaning

Animation should help reading, not fight it. When it adds clarity, desired actions — like finishing a read or clicking a CTA — tend to happen more often.

Designed with ethics in mind

Visitors with vestibular sensitivities or those who've enabled Reduced Motion are your customers too. An inclusive approach here is essential.

Three Simple Principles

1. Support Reading

Well-designed motion supports reading — it doesn't fight it. Thoughtful animations make content easier to consume and understand.

2. Guide Hierarchy

Intentional animation reinforces hierarchy, not chaos. Headlines emerge softly, subheads stay in place — guiding the eye naturally.

3. Create Feedback

Create interactive feedback through subtle motion. A gentle stretch, soft compression on hover — interactions feel responsive.

Trust Works Like Compound Interest

The more respect you show, the higher the LTV tends to be. This isn't about "making it pretty" — it's about visitors feeling their time and attention truly matter.
